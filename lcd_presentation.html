Liquid Crystal Display - Complete Mechanism & Presentation

What is LCD?

LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) is a flat-panel display technology that uses liquid crystals to control light passage. Liquid crystals don't emit light directly but use a backlight or reflector to produce images in color or monochrome.

Light Modulation in Action

LCD Layer Structure

1. Backlight Unit

Provides uniform illumination across the display. Modern LCDs use LED backlights for better efficiency and color accuracy.

2. Rear Polarizer

Filters light waves to oscillate in a single plane. Only allows vertically polarized light to pass through.

3. Liquid Crystal Layer

Contains liquid crystal molecules that rotate based on electrical current. This rotation controls light passage.

4. Color Filter

RGB (Red, Green, Blue) sub-pixels create full color spectrum. Each pixel contains three sub-pixels.

5. Front Polarizer

Oriented perpendicular to rear polarizer. Works with liquid crystals to control final light output.

Liquid Crystal Behavior

Liquid crystals are organic molecules in a state between liquid and solid. When voltage is applied, they align with the electric field. Without voltage, they twist naturally. This twisting action rotates polarized light by 90 degrees, allowing it to pass through the front polarizer. The amount of twist is controlled by voltage levels, enabling grayscale and color intensity control.
